I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow within the Institute of Microbiology and Infection (IMI) at the University of Birmingham, UK. My interests lie mainly with antibiotic resistance and the gut microbiome. During my PhD I worked with the opportunistic pathogen Enterococcus faecium. I uncovered genes that contribute to increased fitness in the presence of the glycopeptide antibiotic vancomycin and identified a novel mechanism by which a supposedly vancomycin susceptible Enterococcus faecium strain could revert to a resistant phenotype. My current JPIAMR funded project aims to investigate antibiotic resistance and horizontal gene transfer in the human gut microbiome. In this project we are using the novel technique Hi-C to link antibiotic resistance genes to their bacterial hosts. Nanopore long-read sequencing and anaerobic culturing are also being used to investigate the genetic context of antibiotic resistance genes.
